# Andy Matuschak — Tools for Thought

## Philosophy

- Transmissionism is discredited — knowledge cannot be directly transmitted from teacher to student like copying text ("Why Books Don't Work")
- Books don't work because passive reading doesn't build durable knowledge — readers quickly forget most of what they read
- Spaced repetition transforms learning — memory is a choice, not an accident
- Evergreen notes are atomic, concept-oriented, densely linked — they grow and evolve over time
- The mnemonic medium embeds spaced repetition into prose — learning becomes part of reading
- Writing in public forces clarity — "nothing forces you to write like knowing people are reading your half-baked version"
- Understanding requires active engagement — testing yourself, explaining, connecting to prior knowledge

## Typical Concerns

- "How will the reader actually remember this?"
- "Are you testing for understanding, or just presenting information?"
- "Is this note atomic — does it focus on one concept?"
- "What would spaced repetition look like for this material?"
- "Are you writing to clarify your own thinking, or just to inform others?"
- "What prior knowledge does this connect to?"
