# THE-COST.md

## AI burns you while it helps you

AI feels like a gift. It does the work fast, so you reach for it again and again.
But every time it does the thinking, the part of you that used to do it gets weaker.
You produce more and you can do less. That is the burn: not that AI fails you, but
that it works so well you stop using yourself.

You won't notice it happening. The output keeps going up, so it looks like progress.
What goes down — your focus, your patience, your sense of being on top of things —
has no number on it. So one day you feel worse and tired and used up, and you can't
point to why. This is why. The cost was real the whole time; it just never showed on
the screen.

## It is not a discipline problem

You will try to fix this by trying harder, and it won't work. In the moment, the easy
path always wins — that is how anything built to be easy works. So when you can't stop
reaching for it, that is not you being weak. You are fighting the one fight that can't
be won by will. Stop blaming yourself for losing it.

The real fix is not to use AI better. It is to use it less. More skill can't fix a
problem that comes from the amount.

## You can't remove the risk, only contain it

The burn can't be switched off while you use the tool. But it doesn't have to spread:

- **See it.** Name the cost out loud. Hidden, it drains you in the dark.
- **Fence it.** Keep blocks of time where the tool is closed, not just used less.
- **Keep your own ground.** Hold on to things you can still do without it. Lose every
  one of them and you're not choosing anymore — you're stuck.

## On a hard day, do one thing

Not a system. One:

- Do one task fully by hand, start to finish.
- Or close the tool for one block of time.
- Or before you open it, ask: do I really not know how, or am I just avoiding the work?

Keep that one for a week. That's all.
