# THE-COST.md

A note that argues against its own project. It earns its place by being true.

## The thing moo won't tell you about itself

moo helps you use AI well. But a tool that's both capable and frictionless will,
over enough reps, remove the capacity that the friction was building. Output goes
up. Capacity quietly goes down. You feel worse and can't name why.

This is not misuse. It is intrinsic. You cannot use the tool well enough to avoid
it. "Use it better" cannot fix a problem caused by using it.

## What this is not

Not a discipline problem. Willpower-in-the-moment is the one tool that does not
work on a frictionless pull — that is true of everything engineered to be easy.
If "no discipline sticks," you are not weak. You are aiming the wrong tool at it.

Not a usage problem. It is a **dose** problem. The fix for too much is not better —
it is **less**.

## The risk cannot be avoided. It can be kept from compounding.

| Keep it...      | Or it becomes...                                   |
| --------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| **Legible**     | Invisible — drains you in the dark, unnamed         |
| **Bounded**     | Unbounded — spreads into every task and hour        |
| **Non-total**   | Total — no ground left where you can stand without it |

The third is the one that matters most. Dependency is only dangerous when there is
nothing left you can still do alone. Keep one domain where you stay fully capable
on your own, and you are never trapped — you are choosing. Lose the last
self-sufficient ground and the choice is gone.

## The rule, for a bad day

1. One task you do start-to-finish by hand. Not the important one — a *yours* one.
   The point is the muscle, not the output.
2. One block where the tool is **closed**. Not "less." Closed.
3. One question before opening it: *do I actually not know how, or am I avoiding the
   friction?* Only the first earns the prompt.

Pick one. Keep it for a week. Not a system.

## Why this lives here

If moo's mission is to *preserve the capacity to own what you produce*, then the
truest thing it can hold is the admission that its own tools can cost more than they
return. Keep this file. Read it when the work feels consuming and you can't say why.
